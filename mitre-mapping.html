What Is MITRE ATT&CK?

MITRE ATT&CK is a globally adopted knowledge base of real-world adversary tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs).
It is built from observed attacker behavior across incidents, malware analysis, and threat research.
ATT&CK provides a common language for SOC analysts, incident responders, red teams, and threat intelligence teams.

Why MITRE ATT&CK Matters for Blue Teams

Helps analysts understand attacker intent, not just isolated alerts.
Enables consistent incident classification across SOC shifts and teams.
Reveals detection gaps across the attack lifecycle.
Supports threat hunting and proactive defense planning.

Tactics vs Techniques

Tactics
The attacker‚Äôs goal or objective (e.g., Initial Access, Persistence, Command & Control).
Techniques
The method used to achieve that goal (e.g., Phishing, PowerShell, Registry Run Keys).
One incident typically involves multiple tactics and techniques.
Mapping only one technique may hide the true scope of the attack.

How Analysts Use MITRE ATT&CK

Map alerts and indicators to ATT&CK techniques to understand attacker behavior.
Use technique coverage to validate SIEM and EDR detections.
Identify missing detections across tactics such as persistence, lateral movement, or exfiltration.
Communicate incidents clearly to stakeholders using standardized terminology.

MITRE ATT&CK Is Not a Linear Kill Chain

Attackers do not always follow tactics in a fixed or sequential order.
Some tactics may be skipped, repeated, or occur simultaneously.
SOC analysts should focus on observed behavior, not assumed attack progression.

How SOC Analysts Use MITRE ATT&CK

Classify alerts and incidents using a common attack language
Understand attacker intent and progression
Identify detection gaps across the kill chain
Improve SIEM rules, EDR detections, and threat hunting
